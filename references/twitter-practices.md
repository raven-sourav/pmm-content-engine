# Twitter/X Thread Practices — PMM Brain-Native

---

## Thread Types (Map to 3 Angles)

| Thread Type | Maps to Angle | Structure | Best For |
|-------------|---------------|-----------|----------|
| **Educational** | Framework/Mental Model | Hook → Numbered insights → Summary + CTA | Saves, bookmarks, authority |
| **Story** | Story-Led with Earned Insight | Hook → Tension → Turning point → Lesson → CTA | Virality, emotional connection |
| **Framework Breakdown** | Framework/Mental Model | Hook → Why old way fails → Steps → Application → CTA | Extreme value, expert positioning |
| **Hot Take** | Opinionated Reframe | Bold claim → Evidence → Objection addressed → Reframe → CTA | Replies, quote tweets, discourse |

Default thread length: 7-10 tweets. Hot takes: 5-7. Deep dives: 10-12 max.

---

## Hook Tweet (Tweet 1) — 80% of the Thread's Success

The hook tweet determines everything. Apply the same 5 hook types from generation-angles.md, compressed to 280 chars:

### Adapted Hook Types for Twitter

**Stat Drop (compressed):** Lead with the number. Cut all context.
- LinkedIn: "94% of B2B SaaS companies admit their brands lack differentiation. But the problem isn't awareness — it's that their positioning document was written by committee."
- Twitter: "94% of B2B SaaS brands lack differentiation. Not because they don't know — because 3 teams wrote the positioning doc. Thread on what actually fixes this:"

**Scene Drop (compressed):** One line of dialogue or action. No setup.
- "VP of Sales: 'Your battlecards are useless.' Here's what I built instead (and how it changed our win rate):"

**Name + Claim (compressed):** Brand name + bold observation + thread indicator.
- "Canva is running a masterclass in enterprise repositioning. 8 things they're doing that most Series C companies won't:"

**Before/After (compressed):** Show the contrast in one tweet.
- "'We help teams collaborate better' → 'Designers ship 2x faster because reviewers comment on the design, not a screenshot.' How to rewrite every headline on your site:"

**How-To Claim (compressed):** Promise + rejection of common approach.
- "How to run win/loss analysis that sales actually reads (not the 40-page PDF). 9 steps:"

### Hook Tweet Rules
1. Be specific, not clever. Numbers > adjectives.
2. Use odd numbers. Specific > round. "$47K" > "thousands."
3. Create an open loop. End with ":" or "Thread:" or "Here's what I learned:"
4. Flag your audience. If it's for PMMs, say PMMs.
5. Front-load the most surprising insight. Don't save it.
6. Must work standalone — people see it in isolation.

## Thread Body Rules

1. **One idea per tweet.** Never split a thought across tweets. If it doesn't fit, sharpen it.
2. **Tweet 2 must deliver immediately.** After the hook, readers decide in tweet 2 whether to keep reading. Make it one of your strongest points.
3. **Each tweet follows a micro-structure:**
   - Claim → Evidence: "Most landing pages fail because they talk about features. The best ones talk about the visitor's problem first."
   - Problem → Solution: "Your headlines are too clever. Fix: write 10 versions, pick the most specific one."
   - Before → After: "Before: 'We offer innovative solutions.' After: 'We cut onboarding time from 3 weeks to 2 days.'"
4. **Vary the rhythm.** Mix short punchy tweets (under 100 chars) with detailed ones (250-280 chars). One-liners hit harder after a longer tweet.
5. **Include specific numbers, examples, names.** "Engagement went from 1.2% to 4.7%" > "engagement increased."
6. **Use line breaks within tweets.** Short lines. White space. Rhythm.
7. **Transitions between tweets** — only when needed: "Here's the thing:" / "But it gets better:" / "The real insight:"

## Close & CTA

The final tweet is what readers remember AND what bottom-scrollers check first.

**Closing rules:**
- Restate the core insight in one powerful line. Distill the entire thread into one sentence.
- Last tweet must work standalone (people quote-tweet it or screenshot it).
- Never end with "Hope this was helpful!" — end with conviction.

**CTA options (pick one per thread):**
- Follow: "Follow @handle for more PMM breakdowns."
- Engagement: "What's your biggest takeaway? Reply with yours."
- DM: "DM me 'battlecard' and I'll send you the template."
- Link: "Full breakdown with templates: [link in reply]"

---

## Hard Constraints

- **280 characters max per tweet.** Check every single one. 281 breaks the thread.
- **No hashtags in thread body.** One max in hook or close.
- **Use 1/ 2/ 3/ numbering format** at the start of each tweet.
- **AI decontamination applies per tweet.** Same word/sentence/structure bans as LinkedIn.
- **Every tweet must earn its place.** If cutting a tweet doesn't weaken the thread, cut it.
- **At least 2-3 screenshot-worthy tweets.** These drive sharing outside Twitter.
