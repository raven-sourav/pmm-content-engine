# LinkedIn Best Practices 2024-2025 (B2B/PMM)

## Hook Mechanics

| Pattern | Template | Best For |
|---------|----------|----------|
| Contrarian opener | "Everyone says {belief}. Here's why that's backwards." | Contrarian angle |
| Bold claim | "{Provocative statement that demands explanation}." | Framework angle |
| Specific story | "Last {timeframe}, I {action} and {unexpected outcome}." | Story angle |
| Data shock | "{Surprising stat}. Let that sink in." | Any angle with data |
| Uncomfortable question | "What if {sacred cow} is actually {reframe}?" | Contrarian angle |

## Post Structure

- **Length:** 800-1500 characters for thought leadership
- **Paragraphs:** 1-2 sentences max. White space is your friend.
- **Line breaks:** Use single line breaks liberally. Dense paragraphs kill engagement.
- **Closing:** End with a reframe, a question, or a single punchy line. Never a generic CTA.

## Engagement Drivers

- **Comments:** Strong POV + inviting disagreement → 3-5x comments vs neutral
- **Shares/Saves:** Framework posts and "I wish I knew this earlier" posts
- **Reactions:** Vulnerable stories with non-obvious lessons

## Algorithm Signals

- **Text-only:** Strongest algorithmic reach in 2024-2025
- **Documents (PDF carousel):** 2-3x reach vs image posts
- **Links:** External links suppressed. Put in first comment.
- **Dwell time:** Longer posts rewarded if content justifies length
- **First 60 min:** Early engagement matters most

## Posting Times (B2B)

- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Best times:** 7:00-8:30 AM, 11:30 AM-1:00 PM (audience timezone)

## Anti-Patterns (Never Do)

- "I'm excited to announce..."
- "Agree? 👇" or "Thoughts?"
- More than 3 hashtags
- Tagging for engagement without relevance
- Humble-bragging disguised as insight
- Wall of text without line breaks
- Generic motivational content without specific experience
