> Extracted from Russell Brunson's framework library. Technique reference only — no voice mimicry.

## The Epiphany Bridge (7-Step Written Version)

Adapted from Brunson's 11-step script. Compressed for written content (LinkedIn, newsletters, threads).

1. **Backstory** — Where you were before. Relatable starting point. Same struggle the reader has.
2. **Old Vehicles** — What you (or the industry) tried that didn't work. The conventional approaches that failed.
3. **The Turning Point** — The event, conversation, or realization that changed your trajectory. (Combines Brunson's Call + Guide + Epiphany steps.)
4. **The Framework** — The system or methodology that emerged from the insight.
5. **The Conflict** — Obstacles faced implementing it. Makes the story believable.
6. **The Transformation** — Results. Before vs. after. Specific and measurable.
7. **The Bridge to Reader** — "Kinda like..." connection. Help the reader see themselves in the story.

**Quick version (LinkedIn):** Hit steps 1, 3, 4, 6 in 800-1200 characters.
**Deep version (Newsletter):** All 7 steps with full narrative, 1500-3000 words.
**Thread version (Twitter):** One step per tweet, 7-10 tweets.

## Hook, Story, Offer

Every piece of content needs all three:

**Hook:** The thing that grabs attention. The headline, first line, subject line, opening slide.
- If the hook fails, nothing else matters because nobody sees the rest.

**Story:** The bridge between hook and offer. Creates emotional connection, builds credibility, establishes desire.
- Told through an Epiphany Bridge.
- The story makes the conclusion feel inevitable, not forced.

**Offer:** The specific thing you're presenting. What they get, what it costs, why act now.
- In content marketing: the offer is the takeaway, the framework, the insight.

**Diagnosis tool:** When content isn't working, one of these three is broken:
- High impressions, low clicks → HOOK is broken
- High clicks, low engagement → STORY is broken
- High engagement, low action → OFFER is broken

## The Attractive Character

Your audience connects with a person, not a logo.

**Four elements:**
1. **Backstory** — Origin story relatable to your dream reader. They see themselves in your past.
2. **Parables** — Everyday experiences turned into lessons. Makes teaching memorable and shareable.
3. **Character Flaws** — Vulnerabilities that make you human. Nobody connects with perfection.
4. **Polarity** — Willingness to take a stand. Repel some people. The businesses that appeal to everyone appeal to no one.

**Three storylines that work:**
1. Loss & Redemption (I lost everything and built it back)
2. Us vs. Them (we do it this way, the industry does it wrong)
3. Before & After (my life/approach was this, now it's this)

## "Kinda Like" Bridge

When explaining complex concepts: "It's kinda like..." and relate to something the reader already understands.
- Creates instant comprehension without jargon
- Especially useful for frameworks and mental models
- Maps to Sourav's teardown/analogy style
