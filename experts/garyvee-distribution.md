## The Pillar-to-Micro Content Model

### Step 1: Create ONE Pillar Content Piece
The anchor. Long-form. Could be:
- A newsletter article (1500-3000 words)
- A podcast episode or video
- A keynote, workshop, or long-form LinkedIn post

### Step 2: Chop Into 15-30+ Micro Content Pieces
From one pillar, extract:
- 3-5 text posts (insights, takeaways) → LinkedIn, Twitter
- 1-2 carousel posts (multi-slide breakdowns) → LinkedIn
- 5-10 short video clips (15-60 sec) → Reels, TikTok, Shorts
- 3-5 quote graphics
- Blog post or newsletter distillation (reverse: micro → pillar)

### Step 3: Distribute Contextually (RE-CREATE, Don't Copy-Paste)

| Platform | Native Format | What Works |
|----------|--------------|------------|
| **LinkedIn** | Text posts, carousels, first-person narrative | Professional storytelling, industry insight, longer text, personal experience |
| **Twitter/X** | Threads, single tweets, quote tweets | Punchy, opinionated, one idea per tweet, links get suppressed |
| **Newsletter** | Long-form article with subject line | Deep framework, evidence-rich, story arc, one CTA |
| **Carousel** | 5-10 visual slides, one point per slide | Educational progression, framework visualization |

### Step 4: Learn From Community Response
Read every comment. What resonates? What falls flat? Feed insights back into next pillar piece.

**Cycle:** Create → Distribute → Listen → Create Again

## Context > Content

The same message needs completely different execution on every platform:
- **Match the platform's culture** (professional on LinkedIn, raw on Twitter, visual on carousel)
- **Respect format preferences** (280 chars on Twitter, 800-1200 chars on LinkedIn, 1500-3000 words in newsletter)
- **Understand user mindset** (LinkedIn = professional growth, Twitter = hot takes + discourse, Newsletter = deep learning)

**Key rule:** If you can copy-paste between platforms without changing anything, you haven't adapted it.

## Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook (Content Ratio)

**Jabs (75-80%):** Give value. Teach, entertain, inspire, share behind-the-scenes. No ask attached.

**Right Hooks (20-25%):** The ask. CTA to subscribe, download, hire, buy.

**The test:** If someone unfollowed you today, would they lose something valuable? If your content disappearing wouldn't matter, you're right-hooking too much.

## The Reverse Pyramid

| Layer | % of Content | Purpose | Type |
|-------|-------------|---------|------|
| **Top** | 70% | Brand awareness — get discovered, build affinity | Broad topics, opinions, personality |
| **Middle** | 20% | Consideration — prove competence, build authority | Frameworks, how-tos, case studies |
| **Bottom** | 10% | Conversion — convert the audience you've built | Direct offers, CTAs |

Most businesses invert this: 70% sales, 20% educational, 10% personality.

---

## Document, Don't Create

When stuck on content ideas:
- Document your daily work process
- Document problems you're solving
- Document wins AND losses (especially losses)
- Document lessons in real-time
- Document opinions on industry news as it happens

Zero creative block. More authentic. Infinitely sustainable.
