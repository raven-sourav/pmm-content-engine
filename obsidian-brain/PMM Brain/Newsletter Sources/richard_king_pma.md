# richard_king_pma

## Focus
PMM organizational positioning, cross-functional influence, sales enablement

## Key Frameworks
- Three-Layer Model of Influence: Signal (the what) → Context (the so what) → Trust (the why you)
- Four Foundational PMM Value Areas: Product Development, Demand Gen, Sales Enablement, Customer Experience
- Stage-Specific PMM Value: Under 50 (prove revenue impact), 50-250 (establish ownership boundaries), 250+ (separate strategic from tactical)
- Rep's Three-Question Filter: Easy to explain? Attaches to existing deals? Helps hit quota this quarter?
- Drip Feed vs Big Bang influence: inter-meeting moments > quarterly planning presentations

## Key Data Points
- 'We lost $2.3M in pipeline last quarter because of X' resonates differently than 'customers want X'
- Typical hiring sequence: sales first, demand gen second, PMM last — creating chicken-and-egg problems

## Contrarian Insights
- PMMs specialize in external positioning but paradoxically fail at positioning their own role internally
- Fighting every product decision means influencing none — reserve political capital
- When a PM is dismissive, invite them to customer calls instead of pushing harder
- One closed deal generates more internal adoption than 50 enablement sessions
