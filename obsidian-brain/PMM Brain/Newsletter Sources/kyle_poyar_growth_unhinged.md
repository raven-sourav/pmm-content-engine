# kyle_poyar_growth_unhinged

## Focus
SaaS pricing, PLG, GTM strategy, AI monetization, benchmarks

## Key Frameworks
- Pricing as Positioning: pricing tells a story about who you are
- Expansion Roadmap: single product + flat fee → ~$5M ARR ceiling; need expansion levers beyond
- Three Drivers of AI Pricing Sophistication: higher stakes/speed, cost dynamics, outcome-based for verticals
- Pre-Launch Pricing Conversations: 'How solving today? What budget? Tech or headcount spend? Approval threshold?'
- Hybrid Pricing Model: combining usage + subscription — dominant model for SaaS and AI

## Key Data Points
- 1,800+ pricing changes in 2025 across 500 SaaS companies
- Credit models up 126% YoY (35 → 79 companies), including Figma, HubSpot, Salesforce
- 53% of GTM leaders see little/no AI impact
- Median public SaaS growth: 30-40% (2021-22) → 15% (2024)
- Early-stage AI companies (<$1M ARR): top quartile growth 250% (2024)
- OpenAI pricing escalation: $20 → $200/seat
- Cursor struggled retrofitting usage-based pricing after launching with flat fee

## Contrarian Insights
- Token costs aren't approaching zero — agentic use adds complexity, driving overall expenses up
- There's no perfect pricing model — every model has tradeoffs, your job is understanding them
- Credits swing: 2025 toward credits, 2026 back toward simplicity
- Incumbents with rich historical data have an advantage IF they layer agentic capabilities
- What ChatGPT says about your product will become top PMM KPI in 2026
