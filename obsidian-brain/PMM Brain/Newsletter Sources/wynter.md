# wynter

## Focus
B2B buyer behavior research, messaging validation, brand differentiation data

## Key Frameworks
- Three Memory Assets: Repetition Engine, Physical Anchor, Problem Owner
- Three-Track Buying Committee: Financial (CFO), Technical (CTO), Marketing (CMO) — parallel evaluation
- Discovery Hierarchy: Peer networks 72%, Review sites 54%, Google 51%, AI tools 24%
- Five-Point Differentiation System: Find 'only we' angle, Pick a side, Use customer language, Maintain consistency, Measure recall
- Five Brand KPIs: Unaided recall, Aided recall, Brand preference share, Perception alignment, Mental availability

## Key Data Points
- 92% of B2B buyers only purchase from day-1 shortlist
- 94% of B2B SaaS admit sounding like competitors; only 6% truly distinctive
- 84% of tech leaders complete research before sales call
- 72% discovery starts in private peer networks
- 52% don't measure whether buyers remember them
- AI research usage: 0% (2024) → 24% (2025) → 50%+ (2026)
- 58% of CFOs do NOT trust vendor-provided ROI calculations
- 79% of CMOs say brand matters for shortlisting vs only 39% of CTOs
- 62% don't trust AI for decisions; only 7% see clear ROI

## Contrarian Insights
- 'When everything is measurable, everything converges' — data-driven optimization kills differentiation
- 9x more content ≠ 9x more memory. It equals 9x more noise.
- Being remarkable loses to being remembered
- Google is a verification tool, not a discovery engine
- CTOs explicitly reject brand-based selection (39% vs CMOs' 79%)
- The bar for differentiation is absurdly low — even basic distinctiveness creates outsized advantage
