# How They Grow (Jaryd Hermann)

## Focus
Deep-dive growth case studies (6,000-10,000 words each) dissecting how specific companies grew from 0→1 and 1→N. Covers PLG mechanics, pricing strategy, platform flywheels, category creation, and distribution advantages.

## Key Frameworks
- The Innovation Stack — interdependent innovations that create non-copyable systems
- Stacked Opposites Strategy — systematically doing the opposite of incumbents on every dimension
- The PLG Iceberg — visible self-serve hiding massive enterprise sales motion underneath
- Negative CAC Distribution — product usage itself creates acquisition
- Sidecar Products — small free tools adjacent to core product that drive top-of-funnel
- Trojan Horse Distribution — enter market through low-friction wedge, expand into real model
- The Popcorn Strategy — subsidize one product to make margins on another

## Key Data Points
- Zapier: $230M+ ARR, $5B valuation on $1.3M funding
- Miro: $17.5B valuation through PLG + community
- PostHog open-core captures developers who hate sales
- Klarna: 150M customers in $260B BNPL market

## Contrarian Insights
- Make your product hard to buy — deliberate friction creates better customers
- Sell features, not benefits — technical buyers want specifics, not vague outcomes
- Gaps in the market ≠ markets in the gap — absence of competition is often a signal, not opportunity
- Stop innovating so much — differentiate by owning what you're NOT

- distribution and acquisition
- pricing and packaging
- platform and flywheel design
