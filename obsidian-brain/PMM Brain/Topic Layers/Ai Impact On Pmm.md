# Ai Impact On Pmm

## Key Debates
- AI as assistant vs employee — most PMMs use it as a spellchecker, not a system
- AI for content generation vs AI for PMM operations (research, alignment, assessment)
- Build your own AI workflows vs wait for PMM tools to add AI
- AI makes individual PMMs faster vs AI changes what a PMM team even looks like
- SEO dying vs evolving — AEO as replacement for organic discovery
- AI slop machines vs AI operators — domain expertise as the differentiator
- Skills/systems vs prompts — persistent AI infrastructure vs one-off conversations

## Common Mistakes
- Using AI for generic content that converges with competitors
- Waiting for vendors to 'add AI' instead of building workflows now
- Confusing AI adoption with AI impact (53% see no impact)
- Not encoding your own taste/standards — so AI outputs sound like everyone else's
- Treating AI as a writing tool only, missing the operational use cases (alignment, assessment, research systems)
- Investing in AI without encoding domain expertise first — produces impressive-sounding empty work
- Treating prompts as disposable instead of versioned, evaluated IP assets
- Ignoring AI search visibility while optimizing for traditional SEO

## Practitioner Wisdom
AI creates positioning sameness — differentiation becomes MORE important. The real gap isn't tools, it's encoding your thinking so AI can execute at your level. Two real use cases: (1) A CLAUDE.md-based content engine that encodes your PMM brain — mental models, evidence bank, voice profile, critique protocol — so every output meets your quality bar without rewriting. Not 'AI writes my posts.' It's 'AI thinks like my best PMM and I edit.' (2) MessagingGPT for cross-team messaging alignment and assessment — instead of 5 teams interpreting the messaging doc differently, AI evaluates whether sales decks, landing pages, and enablement materials actually match the positioning. The pattern: encode your standards first, then let AI assess against them. Without something original to say, the best architecture just produces polished noise.

## First Mover Angles
- Building AI systems that encode PMM thinking (not just generate content) — the CLAUDE.md pattern
- AI as messaging alignment auditor: how MessagingGPT scores content against 12 dimensions so you don't manually review every landing page
- The 14-file knowledge architecture: why encoding your positioning, allowed claims, and evidence vault matters more than prompt engineering
- Session learning loops: AI that gets better at YOUR standards over time (corrections become permanent rules)
- AI for PMM quality control — critique protocols and rubric scoring, not just drafts
- Why most PMM+AI content is indistinguishable from anyone else's (the missing ingredient is your encoded taste, not better prompts)
- The file priority pattern: Allowed Claims > Product Knowledge > Positioning — how to prevent AI from inventing metrics
- AI doesn't replace the PMM brain — it runs on it. Without encoded mental models, AI produces polished noise
