# PMM Value Landscape

## Consensus Beliefs
- Positioning is foundational to all GTM activities
- Customer interviews are essential for good positioning
- Generic benefit-led messaging underperforms specific value propositions
- Your homepage is your most important marketing asset
- ICP focus is better than trying to serve everyone
- Social proof (especially concrete results) builds credibility
- Clear messaging is necessary for conversion
- Product-market fit should precede heavy positioning investment
- Self-service evaluation must match sales demo quality
- Cross-functional alignment is critical for GTM success

## Contested Beliefs
- **Category creation vs. competing in existing categories**
  - Camp A: Category creators argue new categories eliminate competition and command premium pricing
  - Camp B: Pragmatists argue most companies should win an existing category first
  - Nuance: Depends on stage, resources, and maturity. Most should start by winning a segment. Early competitors in emerging categories typically HELP you — they validate the category.
- **Brand positioning vs. product positioning priority**
  - Camp A: Brand-first: perception drives purchase, especially for future buyers
  - Camp B: Product-first: functional differentiation is the foundation
  - Nuance: Not separate exercises — start with product positioning, let brand emerge from authentic differentiation.
- **Specialists vs. generalists in marketing teams**
  - Camp A: Specialists bring depth and expertise that generalists can't match
  - Camp B: AI makes generalists more powerful; specialization was a temporary artifact of technology
  - Nuance: Hire generalists first. As AI commoditizes execution, orchestration skills (Gen Marketer) matter more than narrow specialization. But don't confuse this with 'do more with less' — it's different work.
- **AI positioning: assistive vs. performative**
  - Camp A: AI as assistant — less scary, incremental adoption
  - Camp B: AI as employee/replacement — higher value framing, higher willingness to pay
  - Nuance: Assistive risks being 'just a feature'. Performative has higher expectations. Depends on actual capability and buyer risk tolerance.
- **Data-driven optimization vs. creative differentiation**
  - Camp A: Optimize everything measurable for maximum performance
  - Camp B: When everything is measurable, everything converges — optimization kills differentiation
  - Nuance: The bar for differentiation is absurdly low (94% of competitors are indistinguishable). It's a leadership problem, not a budget problem — 68% need permission to take creative risks, not more money.
- **SEO-first vs community-first discovery**
  - Camp A: Organic search is the foundation of B2B demand gen
  - Camp B: 72% of discovery starts in private peer networks; only 9% of CMOs google a category
  - Nuance: Google is a verification tool, not a discovery engine. Dark social eclipses search. Build community/reference programs alongside SEO, not instead of it.
- SEO is dying vs SEO is evolving — AI Overviews causing 70% CTR drops but organic still matters for long-tail
- AI agent companies should be PLG vs sales-led first — token costs and trust gaps make pure self-serve risky
- Intent data vendors deliver real signal vs most deanonymization is only 42% accurate
- PLG as growth model vs PLG as acquisition channel for enterprise sales — Iceberg view: 60-80% revenue from enterprise deals
- Reduce friction vs deliberate friction — strategic scarcity signals quality
- Features-first vs benefits-first for technical products
- Founder-written content vs agency-delegated — authenticity vs scalability

## Emerging Beliefs
- In a world where features are commodities, curation and editorial choices are the new competitive advantage
- AI creates positioning sameness — sharp differentiation becomes MORE important, not less
- Share of Model is the new Share of Voice — position for AI agents too, not just humans
- The commoditization paradox: AI making code cheaper makes positioning/messaging/distribution MORE valuable
- Pricing tells a story about who you are — it's positioning, not just monetization
- 53% of GTM leaders see no AI impact — the constraint is mindset, not technology
- Without something original to say, the best AI architecture just produces polished noise
- Credit models for AI pricing will swing back toward simplicity in 2026
- ChatGPT's description of your product will become the top PMM KPI
- The Gen Marketer (π-shaped) will replace narrow specialist roles
- Boring consistency beats clever campaigns for mental availability
- Physical experiences create mental availability that digital cannot match
- AEO (AI Engine Optimization) will replace SEO as primary organic discovery channel within 2-3 years
- GTM engineering is a distinct role with $135K median salary — not just 'marketing ops with Clay'
- Prompts are strategic IP requiring version control, evaluation, and reuse infrastructure — not disposable conversations
- Composable MarTech (BYOK APIs) will replace bundled platforms — 60-70% cost savings with better data coverage
- AI makes personality-led growth MORE important — as AI strips voice, encoded personal style becomes the moat
- Innovation stacks create non-copyable competitive moats — interdependent novel solutions that can't be replicated piecemeal
- The PLG Iceberg is the default enterprise motion — 60-80% of revenue from enterprise deals seeded by individual adoption
- Founder-led content as fiduciary duty — financially irresponsible NOT to publish
- Content ecosystem thinking — founder + team + company page as coordinated system
- Savability as primary content quality metric over likes/comments
- Full Stack AI Marketer: one person + AI agents replaces specialist department
- Digital twins of customers (LLM-simulated personas) for pre-launch testing
- Open-core as default GTM for developer tools — code as distribution channel
- Events renaissance — AI saturated digital channels, human connection premium rising
